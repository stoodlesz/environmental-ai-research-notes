# About

This site is a place for research notes at the intersection of environmental geography, artificial intelligence, cybersecurity, and ethical technology.

I am using it to document ideas, experiments, datasets, model tests, and reflections as the project develops. The tone is intentionally practical: part field notebook, part technical lab book, part public archive.

The first focus is coastal dune ecosystems and psammosere succession, with a particular interest in whether satellite imagery and computer vision can help monitor ecological change over time.

I am also interested in medical AI, especially the question of how AI systems might support better diagnosis, research, monitoring, and decision-making without losing sight of the people affected by those systems.

Across both environmental and medical applications, my mindset is the same: AI should be useful, careful, transparent, and guided by a moral compass. I am interested in building with ambition, but not blindly. That means thinking about data quality, bias, provenance, security, robustness, and the real-world consequences of model outputs from the beginning.
